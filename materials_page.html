Materials

Source files: src/Objects/Plugins/CorePlugin/Materials/

Materials describe how a surface responds to light. Each material implements the RayTracer::IMaterial interface, which models the full physically-based shading contract used during path tracing in RayTracer::Camera::castRay.

The BRDF Interface

Four methods on RayTracer::IMaterial drive the rendering equation:

Method Purpose
evaluate(normal, viewDir, lightDir) BRDF value at the surface — how much light is reflected from lightDir toward viewDir
sample(normal, viewDir) Deterministic outgoing direction for the next bounce — no random sampling
pdf(normal, viewDir, sampleDir) Probability density of sampleDir under sample() — weights the Monte Carlo estimator
emission() Radiance emitted by the surface itself — zero for non-emissive materials

The renderer calls sample() to get the next ray direction, then weights the accumulated radiance by evaluate() / pdf() before recursing.

All standard materials inherit from RayTracer::AMaterial which adds _albedo (RayTracer::Color) as shared base state.

Lambertian

Source: src/Objects/Plugins/CorePlugin/Materials/Lambertian/Lambertian.hpp

RayTracer::Lambertian is a perfectly diffuse material that scatters light uniformly in all directions of the upper hemisphere.

  • evaluate() — returns albedo / π, the cosine-weighted Lambertian BRDF
  • sample() — returns a deterministic direction sampled from a cosine-weighted lobe aligned to the surface normal
  • pdf() — cosine-over-π consistent with the sampling strategy
  • emission() — zero (non-emissive)

Suitable for matte, chalky, or unpolished surfaces.

Phong

Source: src/Objects/Plugins/CorePlugin/Materials/Phong/Phong.hpp

RayTracer::Phong models specular highlights using the classic Phong reflection model combined with a diffuse base.

Members:

  • _shininess — float, controls the width of the specular lobe (default: 32.0)
  • evaluate() — combines Lambertian diffuse and specular (R·V)^shininess term
  • sample() — deterministic direction biased toward the specular reflection lobe
  • pdf() — density matching the sample strategy
  • emission() — zero

Suitable for plastic, polished, or shiny surfaces with directional highlights.

Glass

Source: src/Objects/Plugins/CorePlugin/Materials/Glass/Glass.hpp

RayTracer::Glass models a dielectric (refractive and reflective) material using Snell's law and the Fresnel equations.

Members:

  • _refractiveIndex — float, ratio of indices of refraction (default: 1.5 for glass)
  • evaluate() — Fresnel-weighted combination of reflection and refraction
  • sample() — deterministic refraction or reflection direction based on the angle of incidence and the refractive index
  • pdf() — density for the chosen transmission or reflection event
  • emission() — zero

Total internal reflection is handled automatically: when the refraction angle exceeds the critical angle the material reflects rather than refracts.

Suitable for glass spheres, water, or any transparent dielectric surface.

Extending with New Materials

To add a new material:

  1. Subclass RayTracer::AMaterial (or directly RayTracer::IMaterial).
  2. Implement evaluate(), sample(), pdf(), and emission().
  3. Register the class in a plugin's initializer map. See Plugin System.
